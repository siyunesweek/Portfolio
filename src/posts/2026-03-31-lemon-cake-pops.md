Today I am sharing an incredible recipe to make Lemon Cake Pops entirely from scratch. We are going to bake a fluffy lemon sponge cake, prepare a smooth buttercream, and cover everything in a crisp chocolate shell. I made this recipe for the spring dessert competition at my work.

## Part 1: Lemon Sponge Cake Base

### Ingredients
* 1 large organic lemon (zest and juice)
* 110g sugar
* 150g all-purpose flour
* 4 eggs
* 25ml sunflower oil
* 5g baking powder
* A pinch of salt

### Instructions
1. **Prep the oven:** Preheat your oven to 180°C. 
2. **Whip the whites:** Separate the egg whites and yolks. Whip the egg whites to stiff peaks with a pinch of salt and half of the sugar. Set aside.
3. **Mix the wet ingredients:** Mix the egg yolks with the remaining sugar, the lemon zest, and the lemon juice until the mixture becomes slightly pale.
4. **Combine:** Add the sunflower oil and the sifted flour along with the baking powder. Mix until fully combined.
5. **Fold:** Incorporate a tablespoon of the whipped egg whites into the yolk mixture and mix gently. Then, pour the yolk mixture over the remaining egg whites and combine using gentle folding motions so you don't lose the air.
6. **Bake:** Pour the batter into a greased baking pan and bake at 170°C for 30 minutes. Let it cool completely on a wire rack.

## Part 2: Buttercream & Cake Pop Assembly

### Ingredients
* 3 chocolate bars (for melting)
* 160g unsalted butter (room temperature)
* 125g powdered sugar (icing sugar)
* Optional: Almonds for topping

### Instructions

1. **Crumble the cake:** Crumble the entire cooled lemon sponge cake into a large bowl until there are no large chunks left.
2. **Make the buttercream:** Beat the room-temperature butter on its own for a few seconds. Add the powdered sugar and beat for at least 7 minutes until you can no longer feel the sugar crystals and it reaches a smooth, creamy texture.
3. **Mix the dough:** Add the buttercream to the crumbled cake little by little (about 2 tablespoons at a time), mixing with a fork until you achieve a compact texture that holds its shape.
4. **Shape the cake pops:** Form small balls by passing the mixture from hand to hand to compact it, then roll them smoothly between your palms. Place them on a tray and chill in the freezer for 10 to 30 minutes.
5. **Melt the chocolate:** Melt the chocolate bars in a double boiler (bain-marie) until completely liquid and smooth.
6. **Dip and chill:** Dip the chilled cake balls into the melted chocolate using two forks. Let them cool and set on a tray lined with parchment paper. 
7. **Final touch:** Chill them in the fridge or freezer until the chocolate hardens, and you are ready to enjoy your incredible cake pops!
